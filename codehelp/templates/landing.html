CodeHelp is a tool that can help you while you code.

It can explain issues and answer questions about your code. It uses large language models (LLMs) to understand code and natural language queries and respond in natural language as well.

You can:

  • Ask how to start writing a program.
  • Get an explanation of a confusing error message.
  • Ask why your code isn't working correctly in a particular spot.
  • Learn more about specific syntax, functions, or techniques in your programming language of choice.
  • ... and a lot more.

For instructors:

CodeHelp helps your students any time they have a question, and it provides help without writing code for them. It is made specifically to guide and explain, not just provide solution code.

You can:

  • Incorporate LLMs into your teaching in a managed way, offering your students the eductional benefits LLMs can provide without compromising academic integrity.
  • Tailor responses to your class by providing keywords that responses should not use (e.g. sum() if you want students practicing for loops instead of using a built-in function).
  • Gain insights into what your students are working on and the sorts of questions they are asking.
  • Connect it to your LMS, so everyone in your class can log in via a simple link without creating and managing a separate account.
